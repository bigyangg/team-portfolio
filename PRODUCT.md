# PRODUCT.md

**Register**: brand (this is a portfolio + petition, design IS the product)

## Product Purpose

A formal portfolio site that bundles the NGHTT (National Green Hydrogen Think Tank) proposal, the six-application strategy, the 11-person founder team with verified CVs, and the Ministerial Designation request into one place. Submitted to Nepal's Ministry of Energy as a single signed brief.

**NGHTT is a nationally designated public trust.** Not an organization, not a department, not an NGO, not a startup. A trust, registered under Nepal law, with perpetual succession that survives political cycles. Refer to it as "the trust" or "the public trust" — never "the organization" or "the company."

This is not a startup landing page, not a research blog, not a university department site. It is a government-facing petition: serious, source-traced, accountable.

## Users

Primary: officials at Nepal's Ministry of Energy, Water Resources, and Irrigation reviewing the designation request. Likely opens the site once on a desktop in an office, reads it linearly, then forwards the URL or PDF to colleagues.

Secondary: international funders and SAARC counterparts evaluating Nepal's hydrogen readiness. Tertiary: the 11 named founder-team members who will share this link as proof of affiliation.

## Brand & Tone

- **Voice**: scientific, restrained, accountable. Not aspirational. Not "we believe..." but "we have already done X, Y, Z and propose Z."
- **Authority**: institutional, not personal. Names appear with credentials, not headshots-and-quotes.
- **Geography**: rooted in the Nepali Himalayas. Glacial water, monsoon hydropower, altitude. Not generic green-tech.
- **Language**: English-first (for international funders); Devanagari numerals used where dates are Bikram Sambat.

## Anti-References

Things this site must NOT look like:

- SaaS landing pages (purple gradients, bento grids, hero-metric strips, sparkline widgets)
- Crypto / web3 (neon on dark, holographic, glassmorphism-everywhere)
- Climate-tech startups (leaf icons, dewdrop motifs, "carbon-negative" badges, generic "sustainable" lexicon)
- NGO templates (stock photos of children, smiling teams in identical shirts, donate-button CTAs)
- Government bureaucracy templates (Bootstrap, default fonts, generic seal headers)
- University labs (Times New Roman, abstract science illustrations, publication lists as the homepage)

## Strategic Principles

1. **Source-traceable**: every claim links to a CV, document, or government register. No hand-wavy assertions.
2. **Accountable, not aspirational**: when we list 2087 BS targets, they are commitments we can be measured against, not aspirations.
3. **Compositional, not promotional**: this is a brief that happens to be a website, not a website pretending to be a brief.
4. **Quiet by default**: motion and color are used as accents, never as scenery. The Himalaya does the work in the hero; everything else stays out of its way.

## Register-Specific Lanes (to actively reject)

- "Climate-tech startup" lane: bento cards, mint gradients, glass-card-everything
- "AI-generated portfolio" lane: oversized centered H1, three-column feature row, identical icon-title-body cards
- "Govtech dashboard" lane: sidebar nav, data-table chrome, blue/grey palette
